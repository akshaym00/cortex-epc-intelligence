# Project Cortex Pitch

## 1. Fragmented information creates invisible schedule risk

Data-centre delivery teams coordinate thousands of activities and hundreds of contractors across RFIs, reports, schedules, submittals, and email. Individual documents show facts; they do not show propagation.

## 2. Project Cortex creates a living project model

Documents become typed entities, validated relationships, independent events, and a persistent knowledge graph. The graph enables dependency reasoning, quantified schedule forecasts, explainable impacts, and project-specific actions.

## 3. Flagship workflow

A vendor reports a nine-day generator delay. Cortex identifies the vendor, generator, delivery milestone, testing, and commissioning dependencies; calculates baseline-to-forecast date movement; identifies critical-path exposure; and proposes recovery actions with estimated days recoverable.

## 4. Technical differentiation

- AI for unstructured extraction
- Deterministic semantic validation
- Directed graph for dependency propagation
- Baseline schedule arithmetic for forecast dates
- Explainability through stored dependency paths
- Repeatable scale benchmark at 15,000 activities / 200 contractors

## 5. Business value

- Earlier visibility into cascading delays
- Faster impact assessment after RFIs and vendor notices
- Fewer manual cross-document checks
- Defensible, traceable recommendations
- Reusable project intelligence across the construction lifecycle

## 6. Roadmap

1. Primavera P6 / MS Project integration
2. Multi-document provenance and human approval workflow
3. Spec-vs-submittal compliance with citations
4. Graph database and asynchronous ingestion
5. Portfolio-level risk benchmarking
